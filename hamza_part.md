## Hamza's Progress (Mechanical Validation & 3D Printing)

This section discusses the mechanical validation and 3D printing preparation of the robotic arm components. The work focused on ensuring that all designed parts are manufacturable, structurally reliable, and optimized before fabrication.

The process started with reviewing the final CAD models of all components, including the base, shoulder joint, elbow joint, and gripper parts. Each component was analyzed based on its geometry, load conditions, and role within the robotic system.

All parts were then imported into IdeaMaker for slicing and simulation. This step was used to evaluate printability, detect potential issues such as overhangs or weak regions, and estimate print time, material consumption, and cost.

A detailed comparison between components was conducted. The base and shoulder joints were identified as the most critical parts since they تحمل the highest torque and support the majority of the arm’s weight and payload. As a result, these parts require higher infill density, thicker walls, and stronger structural settings. The elbow joint experiences moderate torque and requires balanced strength and material usage. The gripper components experience the lowest load and were optimized to be lightweight and efficient for faster actuation.

Slicing parameters were carefully optimized for each part, including infill percentage, support structure placement, and shell thickness. Special attention was given to support generation to ensure print stability while minimizing post-processing effort.

Additionally, torque considerations were analyzed to understand load distribution along the arm. The base and shoulder joints experience the highest torque due to their distance from the payload and the total weight they support. The elbow joint carries a reduced load, while the gripper is subjected to minimal torque since it only interacts with the object.

Based on these analyses, all components were validated for fabrication and prepared for the next stage, which involves physical 3D printing, assembly, and mechanical testing.
